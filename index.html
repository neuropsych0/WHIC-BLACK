The neocortex displays a laminar distribution. Neurons are structured in six layers parallel to the cortical surface, reflecting input/output segregation:

  • Supragranular (L1-3): Local connectivity and feedback integrations.
  • Granular (L4): Primary feedforward sensory inputs.
  • Infragranular (L5-6): Output projections to subcortical areas and long-range feedback.

Physiological Principle

Excitation-Inhibition Balance

Neocortical networks operate in a tightly regulated state where excitatory inputs are tracked closely by proportional local inhibition, stabilizing activity within millisecond scales.

Circuit Control

Gain Control

Inhibitory interneurons serve as divisive gain controllers, scaling dynamic range and preventing saturation of excitatory outputs during intense sensory stimulation.

Computational Motif

Sparse Coding

Strong feedback inhibition silences weak responses, ensuring that only neurons with high selectivity fire. This saves metabolic energy and increases informational capacity.

Pathological State

Hyperexcitability

Loss of parvalbumin-positive (PV+) local inhibition breaks stabilization, triggering runaway positive feedback loop patterns reminiscent of epileptic discharges.

Synaptic Weight (\(w\)) 15.4 nS (+54% change)
\(w_{min}\) = 0.0 nS (Suppressed) \(w_{initial}\) = 10.0 nS \(w_{max}\) = 25.0 nS (Saturated)
Hebbian Postulate

Causal Correlation

"Cells that fire together, wire together." If a pre-synaptic action potential consistently precedes a post-synaptic spike within a narrow window (e.g. 0 to 20 ms), the causal relationship strengthens the synapse (LTP).

Anti-Hebbian Plasticity

Depression Window

If the pre-synaptic cell fires *after* the post-synaptic cell, the input was not causal in triggering the output spike. The pathway is deemed redundant, leading to Long-Term Depression (LTD).

Biophysical Mechanism

Calcium Influx Rules

STDP is governed by NMDA receptor dynamics. Pre-before-post timing causes a high-magnitude calcium surge through NMDA channels, recruiting AMPA receptors (LTP). Post-before-pre causes a smaller, slow calcium influx, prompting AMPA removal (LTD).
